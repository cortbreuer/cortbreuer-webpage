I'm Cort Breuer, a Cellular and Biomolecular Engineering student at Cornell University.

I've worked on gene therapies for the immune system (Moon Lab/Mauguire Lab @ MGH/HMS), immune organoids (Singh Lab @ Cornell), and lymphatic microfluidic organ-on-chips (Lee Lab @ Cornell). I focus on immunoengineering, systems biology, and computational biology in my studies and work.

Research & Projects

  • Lymphatics-on-Chip

    I design 3D microfluidic organ-on-chip devices that model the lymphatic vasculature in the Lee Lab at Cornell.

    Aug 2019 — Present

  • Immune Targeted Gene Therapies

    I applied exosome-bound adeno-associated viral vectors to targeted gene therapy delivery to immune cells for the development of more efficient immunotherapies.

    Jun 2017 — Aug 2019

    Publication

  • Computational Biology

  • Bioethics & Science Policy
